visitors Naked CSS Day is a day (April 9th) that web designers & developers come together to remove the styling of their website in the name of standards-based design and best practices.

Accessibility and accountability are becoming incredibly important now that people have access to the web almost anywhere. Users deserve to get a functional experience no matter how they get there or when they're using assistive devices.

Removing the styling of your website exposes your content as a computer would see it. No shady tactics or poorly-formed design ideas will function correctly. The idea behind this event is to promote Web Standards. This includes proper use of HTML, semantic markup, a good structural hierarchy, and of course, engaging content.

The project was abandoned in 2009 by a fellow developer and I picked it up as I was (and continue to be) a big proponent of web standards and accessible content. I've lived in close proximity to people with disabilities and I've seen how much sloppy development actually impacts people who cannot browse the internet through traditional interfaces. I have been in charge of it for four years now and it is growing exponentially. It's sort of my baby.
